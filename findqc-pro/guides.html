Search the right way.
Read the evidence carefully.

FindQC describes itself as a QC discovery and aggregation platform. Its official pages document link and image search, while its Academy also explains keyword search. Here is what each route is good for—and what the results cannot prove.

Three documented search routes

Start with the clue you already have.

The official FindQC Academy compares link, image and keyword search. The best starting point depends on whether you have an exact listing, a reference image or only a product description.

01Most exact

Search by product link

Use an existing marketplace, supplier or agent link to target the matching item and its available QC records.

Best whenYou already know the exact listing.
Watch forThis route depends on a valid link; removed or broken listings may stop resolving.
02Visual match

Search by product image

Upload a reference image so the matching system can look for visually similar products and QC pictures.

Best whenThe item is unknown or reliable keywords are missing.
Watch forImage quality, crop and camera angle can change the matches. Confirm the source listing yourself.
03Broad discovery

Search by keywords

Use specific terms together to explore products and narrow a larger set of QC-image results.

Best whenYou are exploring a category, model or product type.
Watch forInput wording and language affect relevance, so broader results normally require more filtering.
Verified on official pages

What FindQC says it is—and is not.

These distinctions come from FindQC's current Terms, Privacy Policy and public product pages, not from assumptions.

01

A discovery and aggregation service

The official Terms list Link & Image Search, Regular & Premium QC, Link Converter, Size Assistant and a Discord Bot among the service's features.

02

Most QC records originate elsewhere

FindQC says QC photos, weight, dimensions and defect records generally come from third-party agents. It organizes and displays that data rather than creating it.

03

Not a seller, forwarder or authenticator

Payments, shipping and returns are handled by third parties. FindQC says QC content can be incomplete or wrong and does not certify authenticity.

THE USEFUL RULE

Observe what the photos show.
Don’t invent what they don’t.

Colour can shift under warehouse lighting. Material feel, smell, internal construction and long-term durability usually cannot be confirmed from a standard photo set.
