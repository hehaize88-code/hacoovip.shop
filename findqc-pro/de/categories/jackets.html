Category field guide

How to review jackets before international shipping

Use the source listing, your recorded order options and the warehouse images together. The goal is to resolve visible, decision-relevant questions—not to turn photographs into a guarantee.

Jackets combine more components than a basic top, so review them in layers: ordered version, overall shape, closures, panels, lining and measurements. Record whether you selected a lined or unlined style, a particular colour, removable parts or a specific hardware finish. Similar thumbnails can hide materially different options.

Photographs can expose missing hardware, visible panel mismatch, damaged lining or obvious marks. They cannot demonstrate waterproofing, insulation performance, breathability or long-term zipper durability. If a functional claim matters, look for credible product specifications or a clearly defined test rather than inferring it from appearance.

01
Buying advice

Three decisions to make before approval

01

Trace every closure

Follow the main zipper from bottom stop to collar, then check snaps, buttons, pocket zips, toggles and cuffs. Confirm that left and right hardware matches and that detachable pieces shown as included are present. A close-up should show the suspected part without hiding its position on the garment.

02

Compare panels and sleeves

Use straight front and back views to compare pocket height, seam lines, shoulder panels and sleeve length. Padded or soft jackets rarely lie perfectly flat, so check whether a difference repeats across images before calling it asymmetry.

03

Check inside as well as outside

Request or use an open-jacket view when lining, internal pockets, labels or taped seams matter. The exterior can look complete while an internal part is missing or unfinished. Do not assume hidden construction from a polished front image.

Visible QC checks

A six-point jackets review

  • Version, colour, size and removable features match the order.
  • Main zipper, buttons, snaps and pocket closures are present.
  • Sleeves, pockets and major panels appear reasonably symmetrical.
  • Lining and internal pockets are shown when they affect the choice.
  • Chest, length and sleeve measurements are read consistently.
  • Protective packing prevents hardware from marking nearby fabric.
02 / Decision rule

Ask for evidence that can change the decision.

Prioritise discrepancies that change use: wrong version, missing hood, damaged closure, major measurement mismatch or visible tear. A minor crease in a padded panel may relax after unpacking. If the issue concerns a hidden lining or closure, ask for that area directly; another closed front view adds little evidence.

03
Common questions

What shoppers usually need to clarify

Can warehouse photos prove a jacket is waterproof?+

No. Appearance does not verify fabric treatment, seam sealing or performance under pressure. Use reliable specifications or a defined test if the seller or warehouse offers one, and keep expectations limited.

Which jacket measurements should I compare?+

Flat chest width, body length and sleeve length are the usual minimum. Shoulder width can help for structured cuts. Compare a jacket with similar thickness using the same measurement points.

Should every zipper be tested?+

A photo cannot prove smooth operation. If functionality is essential and the service offers a test, request the exact closure to be opened and closed; otherwise inspect alignment and visible damage and accept the remaining uncertainty.
