Category field guide

How to review jerseys before international shipping

Use the source listing, your recorded order options and the warehouse images together. The goal is to resolve visible, decision-relevant questions—not to turn photographs into a guarantee.

Jersey listings may contain several teams, seasons, cuts and customisation options under similar images. Save the exact selected version, size, player name, number and requested patches before ordering. When the item reaches the warehouse, identity comes before a detailed quality review.

Photographs can show badge, sponsor, name and number placement, visible stitching and obvious print damage. They cannot confirm breathability, fabric technology, wash durability or authenticity. Use a full front and back view for layout, then close-ups only for details that remain important.

01
Buying advice

Three decisions to make before approval

01

Confirm every custom element

Compare the warehouse item with the written order rather than memory. Check spelling, number, team, season or colour option and each requested patch. A correct main shirt with one wrong custom element is still the wrong configuration.

02

Judge layout from straight views

Use full front and back images to check whether names, numbers, sponsors and badges sit consistently relative to the collar, side seams and hem. Fold lines and angled cameras can distort placement, so ask for the shirt laid flat when alignment is unclear.

03

Review application edges

Zoom in on print or patch borders for visible lifting, gaps, wrinkles or loose stitching. Fine texture can be lost in compressed photographs. If a border looks suspicious, request one sharp close-up naming the exact badge, digit or corner.

Visible QC checks

A six-point jerseys review

  • Team, season, colour, size and cut match the selected option.
  • Custom name and number are spelled and arranged correctly.
  • Requested badges or patches are present in the intended positions.
  • Front and back graphics are checked on a flat, straight garment.
  • Collar, sleeve edges, hems and visible seams look complete.
  • Chest and length measurements are available when fit is uncertain.
02 / Decision rule

Ask for evidence that can change the decision.

Separate an order mismatch from a cosmetic question. A wrong name, number, team or missing patch is objective and should be resolved first. For slight alignment concerns, compare the graphic with stable garment lines in a straight photograph. Do not use the images as proof of licensed status or performance fabric.

Common questions

What shoppers usually need to clarify

Can a QC image confirm a jersey is authentic?+

No. It can help compare visible details, but it does not certify origin, licensing or material claims. The source listing and transaction still require your own evaluation.

What should I check on a customised jersey?+

Confirm spelling, number, placement, team or season option and every requested patch. Keep a screenshot or written record of the exact configuration beside the warehouse photos.

How should I judge badge alignment?+

Use a straight full-garment view and compare the badge with the collar, seam and opposite-side elements. If the result is still ambiguous, request a flat photograph rather than enlarging an angled image.
