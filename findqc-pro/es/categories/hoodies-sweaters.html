Category field guide

How to review hoodies before international shipping

Use the source listing, your recorded order options and the warehouse images together. The goal is to resolve visible, decision-relevant questions—not to turn photographs into a guarantee.

For hoodies and sweaters, the listing image often communicates style better than fit. Start by recording the exact size, colour and design option, then compare flat garment measurements with a sweatshirt you already own. A size label alone is weak evidence because cuts can vary widely between sellers and product versions.

Photos are useful for checking print position, embroidery, seams, ribbing and visible fabric condition. They do not reliably prove fibre composition, softness, warmth, shrinkage or fabric weight unless the seller or warehouse provides separate, credible information. Keep visible observations separate from material claims.

01
Buying advice

Three decisions to make before approval

01

Use garment measurements

Chest width, body length and sleeve length are the most practical starting points. Check where the ruler begins and ends and whether the garment is laid flat without being stretched. Compare those numbers with a hoodie that gives you the intended relaxed or fitted silhouette.

02

Read placement from the whole garment

A close-up can show print edges or embroidery density, but a full front and back view is needed to judge position. Check whether the design is centred relative to the zipper, pocket, neckline or side seams. For repeat graphics, compare spacing rather than relying on one isolated detail.

03

Check high-stress areas

Look closely at cuffs, hem ribbing, pocket corners, zipper ends and the point where drawstrings enter the hood. These areas receive regular pulling and can show unfinished stitching or distortion. Also confirm that both sleeves and hood panels appear consistent.

Visible QC checks

A six-point hoodies review

  • Colour, size and pullover or zip version match the order.
  • Chest, length and sleeve measurements are readable when supplied.
  • Print or embroidery sits consistently on the full garment.
  • Cuffs, hem, pocket corners and zipper ends look complete.
  • Drawstrings, toggles and listed removable parts are present.
  • Front, back and hood views show no obvious marks or tears.
02 / Decision rule

Ask for evidence that can change the decision.

Warehouse lighting can shift dark neutrals and saturated colours, while folded fabric can create temporary creases. Compare colour across several images and look for marks that remain visible from more than one angle. When placement or sizing is uncertain, request a straight full-garment view or a named measurement rather than a close-up with no scale.

03
Common questions

What shoppers usually need to clarify

Can a photo confirm hoodie fabric weight or warmth?+

Usually not. A scale reading may show total item weight, but that is not the same as fabric GSM or insulation performance. Use seller specifications cautiously and treat the image as visual evidence only.

Which hoodie measurements matter most?+

Chest width and body length are the clearest starting point; sleeve length and shoulder width help when the cut is important. Compare the same measurement method with a garment you own.

How should I judge a crooked print?+

Use a straight full-front photograph and compare the design with stable references such as the neckline, zipper or side seams. Fold lines and camera angle can make correct placement look uneven.
