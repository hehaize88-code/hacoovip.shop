ACBuy DIY Order Guide: How Manual Product Links Work

Why some links open ACBuy’s DIY Order page, what a manual purchase changes, and how to submit precise product information without guessing.

Fact-checked July 2026 · 10–12 minute read · Independent guide

A product link usually feels like the simplest part of agent shopping: copy it, paste it and choose a variant. That expectation changes when ACBuy sends the link to its DIY Order page instead of building a normal product page. The redirect is easy to misread as an error. According to ACBuy’s official shopping guide, however, it means the product needs to be purchased manually by an agent.

A DIY order is therefore not a different kind of international shipment. It is a different way to create the original purchase request. The item still has to be bought from the source seller, delivered domestically, processed at the warehouse and later submitted as an international parcel. What changes is the quality of the information available at the first step: automatic import has not filled in all the details, so the shopper must describe the intended purchase accurately.

What ACBuy means by a DIY order

ACBuy’s public DIY Order page describes the service in the context of products from third-party shopping platforms. It gives Kongfuzi Used Books, Xiaohongshu and Youzan as examples. The same page also makes an important commercial distinction: products displayed for shopping-agent service are retrieved from third-party platforms and are not directly sold by ACBuy.

That distinction explains the agent’s role. ACBuy is acting on the submitted purchasing instructions; it is not replacing the source listing with its own inventory record. If the source seller changes a price, removes a variant or edits the description, the purchasing agent may need clarification before completing the order. A manual form cannot make an unclear source listing clear by itself.

The practical rule is simple: treat the DIY page as a purchase brief. Your job is to leave enough evidence for another person to identify the exact item, while avoiding assumptions that the seller has not confirmed.

Why a product link may not import automatically

Automatic product import depends on a page that the platform can read and translate into structured choices. A listing can be difficult to retrieve when it comes from a less standardized marketplace, uses an unusual page format, requires an app session, changes dynamically or does not expose its variants in a conventional way. The official ACBuy guide does not promise one universal reason for every redirect, so shoppers should not diagnose the seller or listing from the redirect alone.

Before creating a manual order, open the source link yourself. Confirm that it still loads, that it points to a specific product rather than a profile or search page, and that the visible listing matches the item you intended to buy. A screenshot is useful supporting evidence, but it should not replace the current URL. Screenshots hide updates and can omit variant menus, seller notices or quantity conditions.

Build a reliable product brief before submitting

The most common DIY mistakes are not technical. They come from sending incomplete or contradictory instructions. A title says one model, the screenshot shows another, the selected price belongs to a deposit, or the remark asks for a colour that the listing no longer offers. The following checks reduce those avoidable errors.

Keep the original source link intact

Use the direct product URL whenever possible. Remove neither the product identifier nor the marketplace information needed to locate the listing. If the link only opens inside an app, test whether the share function provides a web address. Do not substitute a seller’s homepage when you already have the specific product page.

Use a descriptive product name

A useful name identifies the item without pretending to verify claims the listing cannot prove. Include the product type and the seller’s model or reference where relevant. Avoid adding “authentic,” a material claim or a performance guarantee merely because it appears in a social-media caption. The agent needs identification, not advertising copy.

Match price, quantity and specification

Read the listing’s choices before copying the displayed price. Chinese marketplace pages sometimes show the lowest price across several options, while the pictured version costs more. Other pages may show a deposit, add-on or minimum quantity. Select the exact colour, size, version and number of units you want, then check whether those choices change the price.

For clothing, a letter size is not enough evidence of fit. Compare the seller’s measurement table with a garment you own and preserve the chosen size in both the order selection and any necessary remark. For used or one-off goods, confirm that the photos belong to the item being sold rather than a generic reference.

Use remarks for exceptions, not for the whole order

A short remark can clarify a seller-confirmed detail, but it should not contradict the selected variant. Instructions such as “choose the best one” or “same as picture” leave too much room for interpretation when a listing contains several images. If a critical choice is unavailable in the form, state it precisely and keep supporting evidence. If the source itself is ambiguous, pause and clarify before payment.

Pre-submission check
  • The source URL opens and shows the intended item.
  • The product name matches the live listing.
  • The quoted price belongs to the selected version and quantity.
  • Size, colour, model and other specifications agree with one another.
  • Any remark is concise, necessary and supported by the seller’s page.
  • You have saved the current listing details in case the page changes.

Do not treat the submitted price as permanently fixed

A manual order asks an agent to buy from a third-party seller. Between submission and purchase, the seller can change stock, price or domestic delivery terms. If the agent reports a difference, compare the new information with the original listing before approving it. A small change may reflect the selected variant or seller shipping; it may also reveal that the original displayed price did not describe the full item.

Use ACBuy’s live checkout and account messages as the final source for the amount due. Exchange rates, payment methods and processing charges can change, so an independent guide cannot responsibly promise a permanent conversion percentage or fee. If a cost is not visible or explained, ask for clarification rather than modifying the DIY form until the total “looks right.”

What happens after the manual purchase request

Once a DIY order is accepted and paid, the purchasing stage follows the seller’s response. The agent may place the order, wait for domestic dispatch and receive the goods at the ACBuy warehouse. These events are separate from international shipping. A seller’s domestic shipment does not mean the parcel is travelling to Romania or another destination country.

Watch the order status and messages. If the requested option is unavailable, decide whether the suggested replacement genuinely meets your brief. Approving a substitute just to avoid delay can create a more expensive problem later, because international returns are usually less practical than resolving a mismatch before warehouse acceptance.

Use warehouse photos to compare, not to assume

ACBuy’s official shopping guide says that stored orders can be found under “Me” and then “Warehouse.” It also states that QC photos can be checked under the order and that usually three free photos are provided. The word “usually” matters: the current account interface and the individual order should be treated as the final authority.

Compare the warehouse result with the DIY brief you submitted. Check the visible colour, size label, quantity, model details and obvious surface condition. QC images are not proof of authenticity, fabric composition, internal construction or long-term durability. If the detail that determined your purchase is not visible, use the options available in the order interface to request clarification before parcel submission.

For a fuller inspection routine, see the ACBuy warehouse and QC guide. Keeping the original source page, submitted instructions and warehouse images together makes a mismatch easier to explain than relying on memory.

Return decisions depend on the seller and timing

A DIY order does not create a universal right to return every item. Eligibility can depend on the source seller, product condition, category, timing and the platform process shown for that order. Used goods, customized products and listings with restrictive terms may carry additional risk. Do not assume the agent can reverse a completed purchase simply because the product has not yet been shipped internationally.

If the warehouse result differs materially from your instructions, document the difference and act through the available after-sales process promptly. Separate a visible mismatch from a change of mind: they may be handled differently. Never submit the item in an international parcel while a return or exchange question is still unresolved.

DIY ordering does not remove shipping restrictions

Manual purchasing only solves the product-entry step. It does not guarantee that the item can use every logistics route or enter every destination country. Product type, dimensions, weight, batteries, liquids, brand-related restrictions and local import rules can affect the routes offered later. Check likely restrictions before buying, particularly for an item that would be difficult to return.

After storage, ACBuy’s guide instructs users to select a product on the Warehouse page, choose “Submit Parcel,” enter the overseas recipient information and review the recommended shipping options. That later workflow is where the international parcel is created. Use the route and shipping-cost guide for chargeable weight and packaging decisions, or the Romania shipping guide for destination-specific planning.

When a DIY order is the wrong next step

Do not submit simply because the form is available. Stop when the source link is dead, the seller cannot confirm the intended option, the price appears to be a deposit, the item is legally restricted at the destination, or the listing lacks enough information to distinguish one version from another. A manual order can carry accurate instructions across a technical gap; it cannot repair an unreliable purchase decision.

For a first manual order, choose one clearly described item and keep the value modest. Learn how ACBuy displays agent questions, warehouse status and QC images before using DIY ordering for a complicated or one-off product. The best manual request is almost boring: one live link, one unambiguous item, one verified price and a specification that another person can follow without guessing.
