Hacoo Return and Refund Guide 2026: Evidence Checklist for Wrong, Damaged or Missing Items

A practical way to preserve packaging, photograph the right details, choose the correct claim reason and track review or refund stages without relying on vague social-media advice.

A return request is an evidence problem before it becomes a shipping problem. The strongest application does not begin with “the item is bad.” It begins with an order number, the exact problem category, dated photographs and a clear comparison between what was ordered and what arrived.

This independent guide is based on Hacoo public return, refund and after-sales pages checked on July 18, 2026. Those pages describe a 15-day return window for most eligible products, but they also show category restrictions, evidence requirements and different review-time descriptions. Because policies and regional workflows can change, always read the live order page and current in-app instructions before sending anything back.

The safest rule: open the after-sales request first, obtain approval and the return instructions, then ship only to the address supplied for that case. Do not guess a warehouse address or discard the outer package before the review is complete.

1. What Hacoo’s current public pages say

Hacoo’s current Return & Refund Policy says most products can use a free return-and-refund service within 15 days after receipt. It says an application may lead to one of two routes: a refund without requiring the item back, or a return to a Hacoo warehouse followed by a refund under the policy.

A separate help-center policy also describes a 15-day return period but says exchanges are not currently supported in that workflow. It lists conditions for the product, original packaging, accessories, gifts and labels, and says used, washed, modified or unapproved returns may be rejected. Treat “Refund/Exchange” as the name of the order-page function, not proof that every product can be exchanged.

The public pages are not perfectly synchronized. One policy says applications are reviewed within 24 hours, another application page says 24–72 hours, and holiday or promotion periods may extend review time. This is why a careful guide should report the range shown by current sources rather than promise a single deadline.

2. Use a four-step return decision flow

Freeze the evidenceKeep the shipping label, outer parcel, product bag, tags, accessories and invoice. Photograph them before use or washing.
Name one primary problemChoose wrong item, size issue, quality defect, broken item, missing item, wrong parcel or description mismatch.
Apply in the orderOpen the order-page Refund/Exchange function or the in-app return request route and upload the requested evidence.
Follow the approved routeKeep the review result, address, deadline, return tracking, shipping receipt and final refund reference together.

Do not combine unrelated complaints into a long emotional message. A reviewer should be able to identify the product, the claimed defect and the supporting image in seconds. If two products have different problems, describe each product separately.

3. Match the evidence to the problem

Hacoo’s detailed help-center policy lists different evidence for different claim types. A generic photograph of the product on a bed may not prove a size label, parcel shortage or transit breakage. Build the application around the evidence requested for that specific reason.

4. Photograph the unboxing like an investigator

Frame 1: identityShow the sealed parcel, shipping label and full package condition. Keep private details for support, not public sharing.
Frame 2: contentsShow the product bag, tags, accessories and every received item together before anything is removed or discarded.
Frame 3: defectUse a close-up plus a wider image that proves the defect belongs to the claimed product and location.

Video can help when a defect depends on movement, sound, power or sequence, but photographs are still useful because they are easy to inspect and compare. Avoid filters, beauty modes and extreme compression. Use neutral light, keep the original files and do not edit out dates or surrounding context.

For clothing, measure the received garment rather than relying only on how it feels. The Hacoo measurement-based size guide explains how to compare chest, length, waist and rise. For visual inconsistencies, use the checks in our Hacoo QC photo guide.

5. Category restrictions can change the outcome

Hacoo’s public policy identifies categories with stricter conditions. Opened or activated branded phones, laptops, Bluetooth earphones and other electronics may not qualify under the displayed policy. Kitchen and home appliances are described as needing their original seal and unopened packaging. Underwear, cosmetics and some personal-care products are generally listed as unsupported for ordinary returns, although the policy says wrong-item or quality-problem cases can still be submitted for review.

Another help-center version additionally identifies beauty products, underwear, swimwear, leggings, lingerie, jewelry, luxury goods and several electronics as categories that do not support free return in that workflow. The important distinction is between ordinary preference returns and claims that the item is wrong, defective or not as described. Do not assume the same rule applies to both.

Do not test a restricted product casually. Breaking a seal, activating an electronic device, washing clothing or removing a merchandise label can change eligibility. Inspect the order page and policy before opening packaging that may matter.

6. Full refund, partial refund or refusal

Hacoo’s EU after-sales help page says review outcomes can include a full refund, partial refund or refusal for insufficient evidence. It also says the result, amount and route may be communicated through WhatsApp or SMS. Keep the phone number connected to the account current and save the message.

A partial refund does not automatically prove that the reviewer ignored the problem. It may mean the evidence supports only part of the claim, the item remains usable, or the reviewer determined that part of the loss was not caused by Hacoo. The same page says users can contact support again within 15 days after the first after-sales result message when the order still materially affects receipt or use because of objective circumstances.

Respond with new evidence, not the same paragraph pasted again. Identify the disputed finding, then attach the missing measurement, package image, carrier record or listing comparison that addresses it.

7. Do not ship before approval

The detailed help-center policy says return requests must be approved and that the customer-service team supplies the return address. It warns that unapproved returns may not be accepted. It also describes case deadlines after approval, including providing return tracking and a shipping invoice within the stated period.

Use a tracked service and photograph the packed return before sealing it. Save the receipt, tracking number, parcel weight and address exactly as supplied. A return parcel sent to an old address, an address copied from a forum or an incomplete address creates a second dispute that is harder to prove.

8. Separate review time from refund arrival

A review result, refund processing and bank credit are three different events. Hacoo’s policy displays 8–15 working days for credit-card refunds and 5–7 working days for PayPal, while another help page says timing depends on the original payment channel. These are published estimates, not a guarantee for every bank or region.

Record the approval date, refund-processing message, amount, currency and payment route. Then check the original payment account. If the expected period passes, contact the platform’s online service with the order number and refund reference, and contact the payment provider when appropriate.

9. A complete Hacoo return evidence kit

Use filenames that explain the evidence, such as 01-outer-package.jpg, 02-size-label.jpg and 03-chest-measurement.jpg. A reviewer should not need to guess what each file proves.

10. Consumer rights are separate from one platform page

The European Commission’s online-shopping guidance says EU consumers generally have a 14-day right to cancel and return online purchases without justification, alongside legal remedies for goods that are faulty or not as advertised. The exact application depends on the trader, transaction, location, product and exceptions. This article is not legal advice, and Hacoo’s app policy should not be treated as the only possible source of rights.

For a cross-border EU purchase, the European Consumer Centres Network may provide guidance. Before escalating outside the platform, preserve the order contract, seller or trader information, policy version, complete support history and proof of payment. Evidence prepared for the in-app claim is also useful for a payment-provider dispute or consumer complaint.

Final verdict: build the claim before pressing submit

A strong Hacoo return or refund request is specific, chronological and matched to the stated problem. Preserve packaging, identify one primary reason, upload the evidence Hacoo’s own help pages request and wait for an approved route before shipping. Track the review, return and payment stages as separate events.
